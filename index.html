Shahed Ahmed

I am currently working as a lecturer of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). Previously, I completed my undergraduate degree from the same department in the year 2021. Currently I am also enrolled in the part-time MSc. program at the same department.

My research interests include Machine Learning, Signal Processing and Computer Vision. I am always exploring ways to incorporate classical knowledge of statistical signal processing and estimation theory to Machine Learning algorithms and Neural Networks. I am also highly interested in Theoretical Machine Learning. I had the immense pleasure of working with Prof. Md. Kamrul Hasan for both my undergraduate and masters thesis. I also had the priviledge to collaborate with Shaikh Anowarul Fattah on several research projects.

Research

I'm interested in computer vision, machine learning, optimization, and image processing. Much of my research is about inferring the physical world (shape, motion, color, light, etc) from images. Representative papers are highlighted.

COMA-Net: Towards generalized medical image segmentation using complementary attention guided bipolar refinement modules
Jonathan T. Barron, Md.Kamrul Hasan
Biomedical Signal Processing and Control, 2023

We propose COMA-Net for achieving generalized multi-modal medical image segmentation. Our method achieves sota performance across five separate medical image segmentation tasks.

DreamFusion: Text-to-3D using 2D Diffusion
Ben Poole, Ajay Jain, Jonathan T. Barron, Ben Mildenhall
arXiv, 2022
project page / arXiv / gallery

We optimize a NeRF from scratch using a pretrained text-to-image diffusion model to do text-to-3D generative modeling.

SAMURAI: Shape And Material from Unconstrained Real-world Arbitrary Image Collections
Mark Boss, Andreas Engelhardt, Abhishek Kar, Yuanzhen Li, Deqing Sun, Jonathan T. Barron, Hendrik P. A. Lensch, Varun Jampani
NeurIPS, 2022
project page / video / arXiv

A joint optimization framework for estimating shape, BRDF, camera pose, and illumination from in-the-wild image collections.

HyperNeRF: A Higher-Dimensional Representation for Topologically Varying Neural Radiance Fields
Keunhong Park, Utkarsh Sinha, Peter Hedman, Jonathan T. Barron,
Sofien Bouaziz, Dan B Goldman, Ricardo Martin-Brualla, Steven M. Seitz
SIGGRAPH Asia, 2021
project page / arXiv

Applying ideas from level set methods to NeRF lets you represent scenes that deform and change shape.
NeRFactor: Neural Factorization of Shape and Reflectance
Under an Unknown Illumination

Paul Debevec, William T. Freeman, Jonathan T. Barron
